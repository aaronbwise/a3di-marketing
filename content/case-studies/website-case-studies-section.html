MCHN Southeast Asia Data Pipelines

Uncovering Who Is Furthest Behind

Multi-country MCHN equity analysis for Alive & Thrive

Challenge: Alive & Thrive needed to know whether 20 years of progress in maternal and child health and nutrition was reaching the most vulnerable populations across Cambodia, Lao PDR, and Viet Nam. But the data sat in 15+ survey rounds with inconsistent structures.

Approach: A3DI built a reproducible, configuration-driven Python pipeline to harmonize MICS and DHS data across all three countries. JSON config files standardised variables, so the same code processed every country-year combination. The pipeline computed MCHN indicators for health services and infant feeding, applied survey weights, and produced disaggregated analysis by wealth, ethnicity, geography, and education.

Result: The analysis revealed that while overall indicators had improved, progress was deeply uneven. Wealth-driven disparities persisted, ethnic and regional gaps exceeded 35 percentage points in Lao PDR, and health service quality lagged behind access. A&T used the findings for country strategies, donor advocacy, and regional knowledge products. The pipeline was designed as a reusable asset, extensible to new countries and survey rounds.
