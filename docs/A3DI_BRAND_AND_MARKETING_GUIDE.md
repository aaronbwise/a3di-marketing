## 2. Visual Identity

### Logo

The A3DI logo is a **combination mark** — wordmark ("A3DI") locked up with a geometric icon
(ascending data bars within a square frame). The mark communicates trustworthiness,
professionalism, and data-driven insight.

**Rules — never break these:**

- Never use the wordmark ("A3DI" text) alone without the icon
- Never distort, rotate, outline, stretch, squash, warp, or recolor the logo
- Never apply shadows or effects
- Minimum size: 0.75" wide for the full mark, 0.50" for the standalone icon
- Always maintain clear space around the logo
- Never place the logo on busy photographic backgrounds
- The standalone icon (square + bars) may be used alone where space is constrained

**Permitted logo colours:** Black, white, and the brand blues (Blue Crayola / Oxford Blue) only.

### Colour Palette

**Primary palette (dominant — use these most):**

|------|-----|-----|-------|

| Oxford Blue | `#0C1D40` | 12, 29, 64 | Dark backgrounds, headings, hero sections |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | 255, 255, 255 | Text on dark backgrounds, clean space |

**Secondary palette (supporting roles only — backgrounds, data viz, accents):**

**Colour hierarchy rule:** The primary palette (blues + white) must always dominate.
Secondary colours are for supporting elements — charts, dividers, backgrounds, data
categories. The moment orange or yellow takes over a layout, the design is off-brand.

**Recommended pairings:**

- Oxford Blue background + white text + Blue Crayola accents (hero sections, banners)
- White background + Oxford Blue headings + Blue Crayola links/CTAs (body content)
- Gainsboro background + Oxford Blue text (alternate sections, cards)
- Orange Crayola or Mikado Yellow for accent dividers on dark backgrounds

### Typography

| Role | Font | Weights | Usage |
|------|------|---------|-------|
| **Primary** | Montserrat | Regular, Medium, Bold, Black | Headlines, display text, UI elements, large sizes |
| **Secondary** | Lora | Regular, Medium, Semi-Bold, Bold | Body text, long passages, editorial content |

**Hierarchy rule:** Montserrat carries the bold, modern energy at large sizes. Lora grounds
the reading experience in body copy. Do not swap these roles — no Lora for headlines,
no Montserrat for long body passages.

**For web/digital contexts:** The website (aaronbwise.com) uses Source Sans 3, which is
acceptable for web properties. For marketing materials, social media graphics, and
branded assets, always use Montserrat + Lora.

## 3. Brand Voice & Tone

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What it means | What it does NOT mean |
|-----------|--------------|----------------------|
| **Practical** | Grounded in real project experience, specific, actionable | Not theoretical, not academic jargon |
| **Authoritative** | Speaks from 15+ years of field experience with confidence | Not arrogant, not condescending, not lecturing |
| **Direct** | Gets to the point, respects the reader's time | Not blunt, not cold, not robotic |
| **Warm** | Human, approachable, first-person where appropriate | Not overly casual, not jokey, not emoji-heavy |
| **Specific** | Names countries, agencies, methods, tools | Not vague, not generic ("leveraging synergies") |

### Writing Rules

1. **Lead with what, not who.** Open with the insight, lesson, or recommendation — not credentials.
2. **First person is fine.** "I" is natural and expected for a solo consultancy's LinkedIn. Never use "we" unless referring to a specific team collaboration.
3. **Name things.** Say "WFP's mVAM system in Myanmar" not "a remote monitoring system in a challenging context." Specificity builds credibility.
4. **Show the work.** Reference real tools (Python, PostgreSQL, Tableau), real methods (IPC, SMART surveys), and real contexts (anonymised where needed).
5. **No buzzwords.** Never use: synergy, leverage (as a verb), passionate about, thought leader, best-in-class, cutting-edge, holistic, paradigm shift, move the needle, deep dive.
6. **No corporate filler.** Never use: "In today's rapidly evolving landscape...", "It goes without saying...", "At the end of the day...", "I'm excited to announce..."
7. **Contractions are fine.** "I've", "don't", "it's" — these sound natural. Avoid them only in formal proposals and reports.
8. **British spelling.** Use "organisation" not "organization", "programme" not "program" (when referring to development programmes), "specialise" not "specialize". This matches the humanitarian sector norm and Aaron's existing writing.

### Words and Phrases to Use

- "programme data" (not "program data")
- "evidence pathway" or "evidence base"
- "field-tested" / "field experience"
- "capacity transfer" (not just "capacity building" — transfer implies the client keeps the skills)
- "reproducible analysis" / "reproducible workflows"
- "automated pipeline" / "data pipeline"
- "survey design and implementation"
- "data-driven programme improvement"
- "food security monitoring" / "nutrition surveillance"
- "M&E" (Monitoring and Evaluation)
- "mVAM" (mobile Vulnerability Analysis and Mapping — WFP's remote monitoring approach)
- "IPC" (Integrated Food Security Phase Classification)
- "ODK/KoboToolbox" (data collection platforms)

### Words and Phrases to Avoid

- "passionate about data" (show it, don't say it)
- "data-driven" as a standalone adjective (overused; be specific about what data drives)
- "leverage" as a verb
- "stakeholders" (say who: "programme managers", "M&E officers", "country directors")
- "impact" as a verb (say "improve", "change", "reduce", "increase")
- "space" meaning "sector" ("the humanitarian space" — just say "the humanitarian sector")
- "utilize" (say "use")
- "robust" (say what makes it reliable)
- Any sentence that could appear on any consultant's website unchanged

## 4. Target Audience

### Primary Segments

| Segment | Roles | Pain Points | Where They Are |
|---------|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **UN Agencies** | M&E Officers, Programme Managers, Data Leads, Regional Advisors | Internal capacity gaps, data quality issues, reporting deadlines, procurement complexity | LinkedIn, sector conferences, ReliefWeb |
| **International NGOs** | Country Directors, Technical Advisors, Research Managers | Limited budgets, need for reproducible methods, donor reporting pressure | LinkedIn, ALNAP, CaLP networks |
| **Donors & Foundations** | Programme Officers, Evaluation Leads | Need independent evaluation, evidence of impact, accountability | Devex, sector events, peer referrals |

### How They Talk

The audience uses sector-specific language. Mirror it naturally:

- "assessment" not "study" (for food security work)
- "beneficiaries" or "participants" (for people receiving aid)
- "country office" / "regional bureau" (for UN org structures)
- "Terms of Reference" or "TOR" (for consultancy scoping documents)
- "deliverables" (they think in terms of outputs)

### What They Care About

1. **Can you actually do this?** (Proven track record with named agencies matters more than methodology descriptions)
2. **Will you understand our context?** (Field experience in similar countries/emergencies is a massive trust signal)
3. **Will this outlast the consultancy?** (Capacity transfer, documentation, reproducible workflows)
4. **Can you work within our systems?** (Familiarity with UN procurement, ODK/Kobo, standard indicator frameworks)

## 6. Content Strategy

### Content Pillars

| Pillar | Topics | Formats |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **Lessons from the Field** | What went wrong/right on a real project (anonymised). Practical tips for M&E teams. | LinkedIn posts, conference talks |
| **Data Methods Made Simple** | Explaining statistical methods, pipeline design, or dashboard best practices in plain language. | LinkedIn carousels, webinars |
| **Sector Trends & Commentary** | Reacting to new guidelines, donor requirements, or sector reports with a data perspective. | Short LinkedIn posts |
| **Case Studies** | One-page summaries of past projects: challenge → approach → result. | PDF on website + LinkedIn featured |

### LinkedIn Post Guidelines

**Format:**

- 150–300 words for insight posts (the primary format)
- Open with a hook: a surprising finding, a common mistake, or a question
- One idea per post — don't try to cover everything
- End with a takeaway or a question to prompt engagement
- Use line breaks generously — LinkedIn rewards scannable formatting
- Arrows (→) and dashes (—) render well and break up text
- No hashtag walls — use 3–5 relevant hashtags max, placed at the end
- No emojis in professional insight posts (occasional use in lighter content is fine)

**Recommended hashtags (pick 3–5 per post):**

`#HumanitarianData` · `#MonitoringAndEvaluation` · `#FoodSecurity` ·
`#DataForDevelopment` · `#SurveyDesign` · `#MEL` · `#DataPipelines` ·
`#NutritionData` · `#RemoteMonitoring` · `#EvidenceBasedProgramming`

**Carousel post guidelines:**

- 5–7 slides maximum
- Use brand colours: Oxford Blue background, white text, Blue Crayola accents
- Montserrat for all text
- One key point per slide
- First slide = hook/title, last slide = CTA or summary
- Keep text minimal — carousels are visual, not essays

### Post Cadence

| Week | Activity |
|------|----------|
| Week 1 | Insight post (150–300 words) |
| Week 2 | Engage only — comments on sector posts |
| Week 3 | Insight post or carousel |
| Week 4 | Reshare best post to A3DI company page |

2–3 posts per month from the personal profile. Consistency beats volume.

### AI Content Workflow

When using this guide to generate content:

1. **Receive bullet points or a topic** from Aaron about a real project experience or observation
2. **Draft a LinkedIn post** following the voice, tone, and format rules above
3. **Aaron edits** to add personal anecdotes and adjust voice
4. **Schedule** via Buffer for Tuesday–Thursday at 7:00–8:00 AM UTC

## 10. Content Dos and Don'ts — Quick Reference

### DO

- Lead with a specific insight, lesson, or recommendation
- Name real tools, methods, countries, and agencies (anonymise clients where needed)
- Write in first person ("I") for LinkedIn and informal content
- Use sector-specific language naturally (IPC, mVAM, SMART surveys, M&E)
- End posts with a clear takeaway or engaging question
- Keep visual designs dominated by the primary blue palette
- Reference real project experience as the source of authority

### DON'T

- Open with credentials or biography
- Use corporate buzzwords or filler phrases
- Mention the university day job in A3DI marketing
- Use secondary palette colours (orange, yellow, green) as dominant visual elements
- Use the A3DI wordmark without the icon
- Write generic advice that could come from any consultant
- Use more than 5 hashtags per LinkedIn post
- Create content that overlaps between the three web properties
